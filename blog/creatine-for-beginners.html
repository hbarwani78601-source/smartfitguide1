Creatine for Beginners: Benefits, Myths, and Safety

By SmartFitGuide Team

Creatine Monohydrate is the most researched sports supplement in history. Yet, it is also the most misunderstood. Let's clear up the confusion.

What Does It Do?

Your muscles use energy called ATP. When you lift heavy weights, ATP runs out quickly (in seconds). Creatine helps your body replenish ATP slightly faster.

Result: You might be able to do 1-2 extra reps or lift slightly heavier weight. Over months, this adds up to more muscle growth.

Common Myths

  • Myth: It causes hair loss.
    Truth: The evidence for this is extremely weak and mostly anecdotal.
  • Myth: It's a steroid.
    Truth: Absolutely not. It is a natural compound found in red meat.
  • Myth: You need to "cycle" it.
    Truth: No, you can take 3-5g daily consistently.

Who Should Avoid It?

If you have pre-existing kidney issues, always talk to a doctor first. Also, teenagers should generally focus on food and training before supplements.

Important: Drink plenty of water when taking creatine, as it pulls water into your muscle cells.
