Why Weight Matters

Your muscles only grow if you challenge them. This is called "Progressive Overload." If the weight is too light, your body has no reason to get stronger. If it's too heavy, your form breaks down and joints take the hit.

The "Last 2 Reps" Rule

Here is the only framework you need. Aim for 12 repetitions.

  • Reps 1-8: Should feel manageable.
  • Reps 9-10: Should start to feel difficult.
  • Reps 11-12: Should be a struggle to keep perfect form.

If you finish rep 12 easily and could do 5 more? The weight is too light.

If you can't get to rep 8? The weight is too heavy.

General Starter Weight Guidelines

Every body is different, but here are safe average starting points for most beginners:

  • Dumbbell Press (Chest): Men: 15-25 lbs | Women: 8-12 lbs
  • Squats (Legs): Men: 20-30 lbs | Women: 10-15 lbs
  • Curls (Arms): Men: 10-15 lbs | Women: 5-8 lbs

Why Adjustable Weights Win

As a beginner, you will get stronger fast. The 10lb weight you use today will be too light in 3 weeks.

Instead of buying 10 different pairs of dumbbells, adjustable dumbbells let you click a dial to increase weight instantly. They save money and space.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if the weight is too heavy?

If you cannot complete 8 reps with good form, or if you find yourself swinging your body to lift safely, the weight is too heavy. Lower the weight to prevent injury.

Should women lift lighter weights than men?

No. The principles of muscle building are the same for everyone. Choose a weight that challenges you personally within the 8-12 rep range, regardless of gender.

Can I just guess?

Guessing leads to wasted time or injury. Take 10 minutes to do the Test described above. It ensures you are actually stimulating muscle growth from day one.

Safety Tips

Warm Up First: Do 5 minutes of arm circles or bodyweight squats before touching a weight.

Drop It If You Have To: If a weight is falling, let it go. Don't try to catch it in an awkward position.
