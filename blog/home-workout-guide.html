How to Start Home Workouts with Minimal Equipment

You don't need a Peloton or a $2000 rack to get fit. Here is the minimalist approach.

1. The Floor is Your Friend

Pushups, lunges, and planks require zero equipment. Start here.

2. Get One Versatile Tool

A single kettlebell or a pair of adjustable dumbbells can cover 90% of your needs.

3. Consistency > Intensity

A mediocre workout done 3 times a week is better than a perfect workout done once a month.
